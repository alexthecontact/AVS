related paths 4 architects

Portuguese architecture is one of the cultural manifestations with the most significant recognition abroad. It is the result of a long-standing process that began with the dissemination of Álvaro Siza's work and its recognition as heritage that far transcends any notion of border or nation. The following generations seem to have continued this legacy, and today, Portuguese architecture is recognized both within and outside its disciplinary field as a singular activity.
The exhibition showcases the work of four Lisbon-based architecture studios. Four built works from each studio are presented, addressing themes of contemporary conditions such as heritage, reuse, and landscape, as well as the ambition to make the most significant number, particularly in the realm of housing, in response to the global crisis surrounding this issue. An exhibition of Portuguese architects in Japan seems to resonate with Japanese culture itself, which is also characterized by a group of reference architects whose work has informed Western culture since the early 20th century. In this interplay of reflections, the aim is to establish a hypothesis for dialogue and to initiate cooperation processes between both countries, using architecture as a common ground.
The project is a sequence of three events: an installation inside the Portuguese pavilion at Expo 2025 in Osaka, followed by a lecture at the Nakanoshima Museum of Art, and a second exhibition inside the Nipponbashi House gallery, a Tadao Ando-designed building in Osaka.
